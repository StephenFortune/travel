Welcome to Travel Guide Ireland

This Website is designed to help you decide where you can go by taking a look into the Top 5 most popular places in Ireland by County. Many Tourist popluar locations are in the counties above which is why we shall go in depth into why its ranked the way it is.

The Top rated location in Ireland is Donegal with its stoic castle and wild northwestern coastline. Donegal checks off many items on an Ireland vacation bucket list.

Or you could go to Dublin which is Ireland's hub for politics, culture and music.Its approachable and relaxed culture sets it apart from most European capitals.

If the capital doesn't peak your interests you could visit Killarney and become one with nature with the town surrounded by evergreen hills and valley on all sides.

Sligo nicknamed "Yeats County" boasts scenery that insipired one of Ireland's most famous writers W.B. Yeats

Galway offers the best of both worlds for travelers. City slickers can revel in Galway's relatively light but palpable bustle, while outdoor lovers can venture outside the city to see many natural attractions.

Other Locations to Visit  

Cork
Cork is Ireland's second-largest city. Located at the southern end of the country, this city of more than 200,000 residents is famous for being home to Blarney Castle and its Blarney Stone.
Cong
Ashford Castle is no doubt the jewel of Cong, a small village about 25 miles northwest of Galway. You'll need to save up to book a room at the 13th-century castle-turned-hotel.
Cashel
You may be tempted to skip over southern Ireland's County Tipperary in favor of the coastal counties. But Tipperary's tiny town of Cashel is well worth a visit for the Rock of Cashel, ecclesiastical ruins that tower over the surrounding grasslands.
